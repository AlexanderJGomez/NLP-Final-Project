Sport was banned completely under the strict Islamic fundamentalist rule of the Taliban, between 1996 and 2001, with pitches and arenas more likely to host an execution than a training session or a match.
But as the Taliban lost its grip, sport blossomed, with cricket among those leading the way.
In 2008, the team played its first matches in division five of the ICC's global league -- against Japan and the tiny island of Jersey, a self-governing British Crown dependency off the coast of northern France.
The team did enough in the qualifiers to be granted the status of an international one-day team.
But now, what appeared to be nothing more than a dream just a few short years ago has finally become reality.
