The business in Carrefour, near the epicenter of the earthquake and about eight miles from the capital of Port-au-Prince, was operating at a crawl.
A line of Haitians loudly pleaded with guards, putting their hands around the blue iron gate and urging to be let in more quickly to access funds wired to them from beyond the disaster zone.
Basic groceries are relatively easy to find for sale throughout the streets of Port-au-Prince.
There were widespread rumors that the Haitian government would force them to open Thursday, but that didn't happen.
