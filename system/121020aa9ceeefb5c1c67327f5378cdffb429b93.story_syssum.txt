(CNN) -- Washington and Lee University cited slavery's "regrettable" role in the Virginia school's history and announced that it will remove Confederate battle flags from a chapel that serves as a major meeting place.
A group of black law students, referred to as "The Committee," has been communicating with the administration about its concerns, leader Brandon Hicks said Wednesday.
"It's about creating a climate on campus that everyone feels welcome," Hicks told CNN on why he sought the changes.
"I think these are age-old issues that are as challenging as they are controversial and I think there are people all across the spectrum that understand no solution will please everyone.
"Washington and Lee seeks to establish a climate of learning in which we treat all individuals with respect and trust.
