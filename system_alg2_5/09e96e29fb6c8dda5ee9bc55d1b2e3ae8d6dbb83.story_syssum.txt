The world had to do more to prevent, detect and respond to new biological threats.
During the anthrax attacks of 2001, 22 people were infected and five people lost their lives here in in the United States.
On Thursday in Washington and Geneva, we are convening 26 countries to launch a Global Health Security Agenda that will accelerate progress on addressing a wide range of global health security threats.
We can put in place a safe, secure, globally linked, inter-operable system to prevent disease threats, detect outbreaks in real time, and share information and expertise to respond effectively.
