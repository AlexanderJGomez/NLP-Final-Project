Late Tuesday, U.S. District Court Judge Virginia Phillips in California denied the government's request for an emergency stay of her order barring the military from expelling openly gay service members.
The recruiters were told that if a candidate admits he or she is openly gay, and qualify under normal recruiting guidelines, their application can be processed.
Former Army Lt. Daniel Choi, an Iraq war combat veteran who challenged "don't ask, don't tell" and was discharged, moved to rejoin the military Tuesday afternoon.
Choi said he told recruiters he was gay and that there was no reaction or delay in the enlistment process.
Will Rodriguez-Kennedy, president of Log Cabin Republicans' San Diego, California, office, tried Tuesday afternoon to be reinstated by the Marines.
One option is to join another branch of the service, but Rodriguez-Kennedy said he might speak with Marine officers or get legal help.
CNN called several recruiting stations in New York and Chicago.
