Tokyo is a huge and often confusing city for travelers, but with these tips you needn't be daunted by the Japanese capital.
Ideally, have a map of your destination, or at least an address written in Japanese.
ATMs in busy areas stay open until 9 p.m. and most are closed at weekends, although Citibank has 24-hour ATMs.
100 Yen shops These bargain stores are hugely popular in Tokyo, selling everything from food to toiletries for ¥100 plus ¥5 tax -- about $1.
- The Japanese don't pour sauces onto their rice.
- It's a faux pas be seen eating in public -- especially on a train.
