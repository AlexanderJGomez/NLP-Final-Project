"There are more than a thousand, 1,200 houses, at least, that were totally flattened, and others in bad shape," Tocopilla Mayor Luis Moyano said in an interview that aired on Radio Cooperativo.
Places that could be used as shelters, such as schools and gyms, were  damaged in the quake, the mayor said.
Paula Saez with the aid organization World Vision told CNN she was on a treacherous drive attempting to reach Tocopilla.
The government's Office of National Emergency reported that two women had died and others were injured in the city.
Repair work on the 793-meter (2,600-foot) Pedro Galleguillos tunnel, completed in 1994, began on October 1 and was to be finished early next year.
Chile has been the scene of hundreds of strong earthquakes throughout history, including the largest one of the 20th century on May 22, 1960.
A magnitude 9.0 earthquake in what was then southern Peru but is now northern Chile killed 25,000 people in 1868.
