Tairee, a student at the University of Ghana, is proud to wear traditional beads.
Masoom, another University of Ghana student, said he thought women looked good in beads.
"They [women] are more African when they wear their beads than usual.
Beads have a long history in Ghana.
While beads are no longer used as currency, they are still valuable to Ghana.
But the new-found popularity of beads isn't just about giving old materials a new lease of life -- it's about breathing new life into traditional ideas.
"They [the beads] mean so much to me," Tairee told CNN.
