No voting system is perfect.
Voting delays are notorious.

Lines were lengthy in St. Louis City because in the United States, almost uniquely, local governments choose how voting is cast and counted.
The voting would have been overseen by a national election commission, not by local judges, who might be nonpartisan -- but who very well might not.
The American voting system too is an exception: It is the most error-prone, the most susceptible to fraud, the most vulnerable to unfairness and one of the least technologically sophisticated on earth.
