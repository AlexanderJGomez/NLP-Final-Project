The dirty, wet conditions of the landfill attracted the endangered stork, and the stork attracted Bouldry.
The greater adjutant stork initially drew Bouldry to Boragaon, but he became connected with the people.
"They don't look at the things they're doing as being unsanitary or unhealthy or unsafe," Bouldry said.
Some of the children living in Boragaon go to school on scholarship, but most of them work in the landfill to provide for their families.
"I found that the landfill community is content," Bouldry said.
