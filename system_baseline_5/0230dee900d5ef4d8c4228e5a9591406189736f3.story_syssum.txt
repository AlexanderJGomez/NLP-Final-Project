No word can foil him, as he proved Thursday when he beat out other Scripps National Spelling Bee contestants by spelling "knaidel" correctly.
He was eliminated after misspelling words with German roots.
"I thought that the German curse had turned into a German blessing," he said.
Eleven million schoolchildren participated in preliminaries leading up to the national contest this week.
Of those, 281 children made the trip to Oxon Hill, Maryland, outside Washington, for the national bee.
CNN's Athena Jones and Michael Pearson contributed to this report.
