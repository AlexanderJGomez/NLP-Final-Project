(CNN) -- Cancer has surpassed heart disease to become the leading cause of death among Hispanics in the United States, according to an American Cancer Society report released Monday.
Among non-Hispanic whites and African-Americans, heart disease remains the leading cause of death, according to Monday's American Cancer Society report, the fifth.
Hispanics do have higher diagnosis and death rates from cancers of the stomach, liver, cervix and gallbladder.
For example, it is known that Mexicans in the United States tend to have lower cancer rates than Puerto Ricans.
Since 2000, the cancer incidence rate (risk of cancer diagnosis) declined by 1.75% per year among men and 0.3% per year among women.
This compares with declines of 1.5% and 1.3% among non-Hispanic men and women, respectively.
Current data indicate that among Hispanics, 43% of women and 34% of men are obese.
