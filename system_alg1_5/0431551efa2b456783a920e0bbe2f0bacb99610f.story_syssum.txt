Some 150 Tibeto-Burman languages are spoken in India alone, but no other language has been identified as closely related, said Harrison, an associate professor of linguistics at Swarthmore College in Swarthmore, Pennsylvania.
Harrison and another National Geographic Fellow, Greg Anderson, led the expedition, called Enduring Voices, which brought Koro to light.
It is a language hotspot where there is room to study rich, diverse languages, many unwritten or documented.
"We were finding something that was making its exit, was on its way out," Anderson said.
"They sound as different as, say, English and Japanese."
By 2100, it is estimated that more than half of the 6,910 languages spoken on earth will vanish.
