Germany looks set to introduce a national minimum wage for the first time, after months of fierce debates.
The main argument against the minimum wage is that it destroys jobs, harming those it sets out to help.
But this does not happen at the current levels of minimum wages in many countries.
So yes, the minimum wage does help low-paid workers -- it raises their earnings without harming their job prospects.
Many people earning minimum wage, such as students, are not living in poor households.
But it seems likely we may be about to find out.
