An international team of researchers from Chicago, New York and Bangladesh followed 12,000 people over the past decade, monitoring their arsenic intake and mortality rates from contaminated wells.
Stretch that over the entire population that takes its water from wells, and the impact is daunting.
And unfortunately, it is also found in abundance in the soil and rock in Bangladesh.
The authors hope for more study, and a long term plan to deal with the damage already done.
