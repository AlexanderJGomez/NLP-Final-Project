It's only fair.
I hear their voices saying, "Play fair," "Share (my toys) and play fair," "Don't cheat.
"Don't fight, play fair."
In my early 30s, I worked for someone who said, "Fair is when I win."
Fair had been retooled.
Some heard the same words and thought, "Fair is when I win."
How else can we explain board members approving such bonuses, saying "it's only fair"?
Some were indifferent to health care reform, while others were vehemently against it.
For me, it was personal.
It really scares me that "fair," as I know it, may have gone for good.
As a teenager, I fell in love with the writings of the brilliant poet Langston Hughes (1902-67).
God grant me strength to fight for what is fair.
