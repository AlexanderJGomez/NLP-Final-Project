Outside the courtroom, clashes broke out between police and families of those killed in the uprising that led to Mubarak's ouster.
Another man, Mohamed Yaseen, tried unsuccessfully to push his way inside court.
Police chased the protesters and family members, beating some and arresting others.
Mubarak was wheeled into court on a gurney, flanked by nurses.
In addition to accusations of ordering the killing of protesters, Mubarak faces corruption charges.
Also expected to appear Monday with Mubarak were his two sons, Alaa and Gamal.
Meanwhile, Cairo's district courts have indicated they are considering filing individual charges against Mubarak for every person killed by his forces in the uprising.
