Now a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Lamu was once the most important international trade center in East Africa -- as is evidenced by the Arab, Indian, Persian and European influences in the local architecture.
"The Arabs were here, the Indians were here, the Chinese," says local historian Mohamed Ali.
Local artisan Swabry Maawy, for instance, recalls the difficulty he had in procuring his dream house.
It was very expensive for me," he says.
