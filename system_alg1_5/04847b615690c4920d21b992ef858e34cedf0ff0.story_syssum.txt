McCullough, known to his fans as "the Razor," didn't just learn to fight.
"I would like to share with other kids that maybe if they are going through issues of being bullied, or they are a bully, it's a place that they can go to to work that stuff out," he says.
"We are guys that fight for a living, and we could obviously be mistaken for bullies, but we are nothing of the sort," McCullough says.
For some of these fighters, the struggle with bullying is personal.
"I have a 2-year-old son, and I started to think about it," he says.
