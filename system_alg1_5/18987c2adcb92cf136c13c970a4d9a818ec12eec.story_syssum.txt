Larson produced many popular and cult favorite shows in the late 1970s and 1980s, including "Magnum, P.I.," "Knight Rider" (with its talking car), "Battlestar Galactica" (with its space-age hero Starbuck), "The Fall Guy," "Quincy, M.E."
He started in television in the 1960s, working on "The Fugitive," "Alias Smith and Jones" and "The Six Million Dollar Man."
Among them was Ronald D. Moore, who rebooted "Battlestar": "Goodbye Glen Larson and thank you for creating one of the shows from my childhood that changed my life.
Without him there'd be no KITT & Michael."
