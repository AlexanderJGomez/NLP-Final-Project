ALS is usually fatal within a few years, but Hawking has lived with the disease since he was diagnosed in 1963.
"I try to lead as normal a life as possible, and not think about my condition, or regret the things it prevents me from doing, which are not that many," he wrote.
Sharon Matland, vice president of patient services for the ALS Association, told CNN Monday, "He is living an amazing life."
Hawking was born in Oxford, England, on a date auspicious for his future career: January 8, 1942 -- the 300th anniversary of the death of astronomer and physicist Galileo Galilei.
Hawking has guest-starred, as himself, on "Star Trek: The Next Generation" and "The Simpsons."
