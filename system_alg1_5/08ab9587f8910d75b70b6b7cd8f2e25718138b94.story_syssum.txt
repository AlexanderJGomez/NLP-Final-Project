Meanwhile, as much as 10 feet of additional snow is on tap for the Sierra Nevada, doubling the amount that's fallen in an epic storm on the cusp of the winter solstice.
Mudslides forced officials to close a portion of State Route 1, also known as the Pacific Coast Highway, in Ventura County from just north of the Los Angeles County line to Oxnard, according to the California Department of Transportation.
The danger of mudslides will probably intensify, CNN meteorologist Ivan Cabrera said.
"Storm totals of 5 to 10 feet above 7,000 feet are likely," the weather service said, and periods of heavy snow will continue through Monday.
On Monday, Sarah Butner of Orange County, California, was visiting family in Mammoth Lakes in the central part of the state.
"The decks have a good 5 feet of snow on them," and there's flooding at the end of the neighborhood, she said.
