Christian Lusardi, 42, of Fayetteville, North Carolina, flushed $2.7 million worth of counterfeit poker chips down the toilet in a room at Harrah's Resort and Casino in Atlantic City, New Jersey state police said.
Soon after, the tournament was canceled.
Police say Lusardi introduced the counterfeit chips into the tournament on multiple occasions.
He was being held on $300,000 bail, with no option to pay 10%, at the Atlantic City Jail, police said.
