"This is a sacred place," says Raphael Mnkhati, a guide from the Kamberg Rock Art Center.
"It's hard to date these paintings," Raphael explains.
The delicate lines of the figures were etched with twigs, porcupine quills and ostrich feathers.
"You are not allowed to touch anything, or to collect anything here, it's a like a church," Raphael tells us.
"The Bushmen are very special," he muses while looking out over the wide landscape that stretches out below, "because they were living here, peacefully, in the mountains.
We are all from the same origins.
