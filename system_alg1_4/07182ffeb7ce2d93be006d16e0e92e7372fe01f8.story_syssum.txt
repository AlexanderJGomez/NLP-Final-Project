Men staying in an airport warehouse say they paid recruiters to take them to Iraq, believing they'd get jobs.
Many have questions about their visas and status in Iraq.
Asked if their governments were helping them, the men said, "Nothing, nothing."
Some Ugandan men said the Iraqi police handcuffed and beat them.
KBR was not involved in recruiting the men.
They live off food donated by Iraqi workers, and say the men who brought them to Iraq have disappeared.
The men in the makeshift camp said their immigration status is in limbo.
The world organization told CNN it is aware of the situation and is figuring out how to assist the men.
"It's not fair," one said.
