(CNN) -- The Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation announced Wednesday it will donate $50 million to help fight the Ebola outbreak in West Africa.
The World Health Organization said Monday the rapid spread of the virus in Liberia shows no sign of slowing.
USAID also announced it will make $75 million in extra funds available.
The new funds will pay for transportation and support to send 100 more health care workers to help fight the epidemic.
On Sunday, President Barack Obama said the Ebola outbreak needs to be a "national security priority."
CNN's Betsy Klein contributed to this story.
