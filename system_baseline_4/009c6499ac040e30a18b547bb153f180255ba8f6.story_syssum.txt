Many people in Tacloban are still living in tents or other rudimentary structures more than a year after Haiyan.
Yet some like Lucrecia Simbajon, 58, another resident of Magallanes, would jump at the chance of a new home if only she was offered one.
She and her family are among hundreds who have spent the last few days camped out at the local Roman Catholic "Redemption" church.
Her house is located in an area declared a "no build zone" because it was so close to the coast and therefore vulnerable to storm surges.
