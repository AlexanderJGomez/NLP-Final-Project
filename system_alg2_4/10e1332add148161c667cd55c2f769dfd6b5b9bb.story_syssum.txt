The report was put together by an external group of 11 experts in biosafety, laboratory science and research.
One incident occurred in June when dozens of employees in a bioterrorism lab working with the deadly anthrax virus, were at risk because of a failure to properly follow sterilization techniques.
The four-page report summarizes the groups findings and offers recommendations following visits to CDC labs, meetings with CDC staff and a survey about the laboratory safety culture at CDC.
To that point, the committee says in the report, "The CDC must not see itself as special.

We brought this group of external experts together over the summer to assist us with identifying and implementing solutions, of which many are already underway."
