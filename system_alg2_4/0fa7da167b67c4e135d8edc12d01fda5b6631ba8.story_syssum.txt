(CNN)President Barack Obama will renew his push for paid sick days and paid family leave for the millions of American workers who don't have either during a visit to Baltimore on Thursday, according to Valerie Jarrett, senior adviser to the President.
"The last thing we should do is add guilt, fear, and financial hardship on working parents as they try to do what's right -- while keeping their job," she added.
The President will also announce programs to help cities and states provide paid leave to American workers.
The push for paid sick days and paid leave stems, in part, from last year's first-ever White House Summit on Working Families, said Jarrett.
"The conversation we had that day was only the beginning.
