Climate change is not the only challenge the new president will have to tackle, though.

Nasheed, a 41-year-old one-time journalist, was among the fiercest critics of former President Maumoon Abdul Gayoom, who came to power in 1978 and ruled the Maldives for 30 years.
Nasheed was arrested several times in the last 15 years and held as a political prisoner.
But critics said Gayoom instituted democratic reforms -- and legalized political parties -- only after violent protests in 2004 and 2005.
"No other citizens in the world in modern times have changed a 30-year-old regime so peacefully," Nasheed said in address.
