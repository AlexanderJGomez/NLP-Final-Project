The 75% tax will apply to players who earn more than €1 million ($1.38 million) a year.
The UCPF claims the tax will make French clubs less competitive against European rivals in countries which have lower taxes.
The UCPF, which says it pays €130 million ($179.5 million) to amateur sports each year as an act of "solidarity," claims the tax will cost jobs in the French football industry -- which employs 25,000 people.
Big-spending champion Paris Saint-Germain, which has invested more than €200 million ($276 million) in players since being bought by a Qatari consortium in 2011, may have to pay just under half the clubs' €44 million bill according to reports estimating its salary bills.
