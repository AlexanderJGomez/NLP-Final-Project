Normal might be that man in the corner, wearing a T-shirt, shorts and sneakers throwing balls in the air.
Moments later, he's juggling six or seven orange rings (they move so fast, it looks like a blur) and then does a pirouette -- while all the rings are in the air -- and then catches them on his arm.
He was a juggler.
The show went looking for him.
"Juggling is something that is so delicate, you have to have a really good feel, you can lose that in minutes," he said.
But he rarely misses -- at least not in his act.
In fact, I have done this a few times," he said.
