Her stage career began in 1951 in her native Sydney in a production of "Judith" by Eugene Goossens, according to a spokesman with her recording company, Decca Records.
Her record company credits her with rising to international stardom in 1959 when she appeared in Franco Zefirelli's Covent Garden production of Donizetti's "Lucia di Lammermoor."
In 1960, during Venice, Italy, performances of Handel's "Alcina," a journalist nicknamed her "La Stupenda."
