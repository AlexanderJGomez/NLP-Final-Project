Military planners are developing technologies for autonomous drones, aircraft that are supposedly "intelligent" and can make their own decisions on when to unleash lethal force.
The development of drone weapons raises profound moral questions about the future of war.
The use of drone aircraft perpetuates the illusion that military force is an effective means of countering terrorism and resolving political differences.
Many important legal questions have been raised about drone strikes.
The U.S. government arguably has legal authority to conduct military operations in Afghanistan, based on the original congressional authorization adopted after 9/11.
Since the drone war began in Pakistan in 2004, more than 2,000 people have been killed in these strikes, with as few as 386 and as many as 775 civilians among the dead, including as many as 170 children.
