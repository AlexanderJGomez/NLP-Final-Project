What Hall-Trujillo came up with was Birthing Project USA.
Of every 1,000 babies born in the United States, at least six (6.14) do not survive their first year of life, according to the CIA World Factbook.
Hall-Trujillo started the project as an experiment while working for the state in 1988.
The experience moved Hall-Trujillo to quit her job and work on the Birthing Project full time.
"We've been doing this long enough now that you can hear a  child -- 20 years old -- say, 'I was born into the Birthing Project,' " Hall-Trujillo said.
"All of us want to see our babies born as healthy as possible to families who are prepared to care for them," Hall-Trujillo said.
